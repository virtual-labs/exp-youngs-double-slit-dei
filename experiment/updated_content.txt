2.	Double-Slit (Young‚Äôs) Interference (Classical Wave: Water)

1.	Particle (photon)

3. Electron Double-Slit Experiment (Quantum Theory):
This modern experiment shows that matter also behaves like waves (matter waves or de Broglie waves).
i.	Key Observations
‚Ä¢	Electrons fired one at a time still produce an interference pattern, which shows electrons behave like probability waves.
‚Ä¢	If we measure which slit the electron passes through (with detector):
o	The interference pattern disappears.
o	Only two clump patterns remain (each corresponding to one slit). 
o	The electron behaves like a classical particle.
Wave‚ÄìParticle Duality:
‚Ä¢	 Electrons behave like particles when detected.
‚Ä¢	 But behave like waves when propagating.
ii.	Implications
‚Ä¢	 Supports de Broglie hypothesis.
‚Ä¢	 Demonstrates quantum superposition.
‚Ä¢	 Shows observer effect / wave function collapse.
‚Ä¢	 Reveals that nature at the microscopic level is probabilistic, not deterministic.

This guide outlines the steps to perform virtual experiments comparing Classical Particles, Classical Waves, and Quantum Objects.
1. Double Slit ‚Äì Classical Particles (Sand)
Objective
To observe how particles such as sand pass through two slits and form a distribution pattern on the screen.
Steps
1.	Open Experiment: Select "Experiment 1: Classical Particles‚ÄìSand".
2.	Observe the Setup:
o	A sand container at the top.

o	A screen at the bottom.
3.	Action: Click ‚ÄúStart Sand Flow‚Äù.

3.1 Quantum Object with Detector 
Objective
To study the effect of observation on quantum interference (The Observer Effect).
Steps
1.	Open Experiment: Select "Quantum Particle with Detector".
2.	Observe the Setup: Detectors are now placed at the slits to track which slit each particle passes through.
3.	Configure:

Expected Observation: When the slit is measured, the interference disappears. This proves the Observer Effect ‚Äî measurement collapses the wave function.
